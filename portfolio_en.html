Case 01

Gvalt — rebranding a growing fashion label

Role

Branding Producer

ONY · 2026

Client

Family-run menswear brand

Stakeholders

Founders (a married couple)

Duration

3–4 months

Links

View on ONY · Sostav rating

Context & brief

Gvalt had grown, moved onto marketplaces, and broadened its audience — the brand had entered a new stage, and its old graffiti-aesthetic identity no longer reflected either the quality of the product or the new positioning. A rebrand was needed: to build a cohesive, mature image — from strategy and brand platform to a new visual language and communication system.

It was a complex project: strategy was handled by the Signal by ONY unit, and the visuals by the branding team (ONY). There was also a hard external deadline — the launch of the new collection and identity at Russian Fashion Week.

What I did & the decisions I made
  • Kept strategy and visuals connected: the strategist who authored the chosen positioning sat in on internal identity reviews, so the visual language mapped precisely onto the brand platform.
  • Resolved creative disputes through choice: the team prepared three identity concepts and the client picked one.
  • For an unconventional task (the concept called for a handwritten logo), I organized a creative workshop based on the art director’s idea: I invited the founder into the office, and together with the art director and designer he painted logos and graphics by hand. The final logo was assembled from the best sketches. This delivered two things at once — lively, authentic material and the founder’s buy-in: having become a co-author, he readily accepted the result.
  • Structured the phases around the fixed Russian Fashion Week deadline so the brand would be ready in time for the show.
Results
  • Strategy, brand platform, visual language (including the handwritten logo from the workshop), brand book, and communication system — all ready for the Russian Fashion Week premiere.
  • The new visual system was rolled out across communications, digital channels, and packaging.
  • The brand moved from a small family project to a mature fashion label with a voice of its own.

MTS Web Services — identity for a bigtech holding

Role

Branding Producer

ONY · 2025

Client

MTS Web Services — the bigtech arm of MTS, 100+ IT services

Stakeholders

MTS brand team, product teams (3–5 approvers)

Duration

6+ months

Links

View on ONY · Sostav

Context & brief

MWS brought together MTS’s key IT assets — cloud services, AI products, and platform solutions. Before the project they all existed separately: there was no unified visual system, and the scale of the company didn’t come through behind the products. The task was to build a scalable 3D identity that would unite the directions under one brand while giving each product its own face — all for two very different audiences: IT specialists and top management.

What I did & the decisions I made
  • Broke the large project into 6 directions (cloud, AI, data analytics, IT tools, business applications, cybersecurity) and built the timeline so they could run in parallel without losing momentum.
  • Deadlines were tight and the scope was large — so I monitored the team’s workload in real time and brought in additional designers when needed. That made it possible to hold quality where otherwise either quality or timing would have had to give.
  • Set up an approval process with 3–5 people on the client side. When edits contradicted one another, I brought the team into a meeting, explained the logic behind the decisions, and helped the client reach a common ground — instead of endless rounds of revisions.
  • Separately organized the project’s submission to industry awards on a very tight schedule: assembling the case and producing the video presentation, including sound design.
Results
  • A unified visual system across 6 directions, a brand book, 30+ 3D illustrations, merch, and an exhibition stand.
  • The style was presented at the True Tech Day 2025 festival.
  • The style was rolled out across the holding’s products, merch, and communications.

Museum of Soviet Arcade Machines — rebranding a cultural space

Role

Branding Producer

ONY · 2025

Client

Museum of Soviet Arcade Machines — an interactive cultural space

Stakeholders

Founder, museum manager, PR department

Duration

6+ months

Links

View on ONY · Sostav

Context & brief

The museum had grown, and its old visual system could no longer keep up. A rebrand was needed — a system that works both in digital and offline and conveys the museum’s character: engineering-minded, enthusiastic, and curiosity-sparking. The challenge was to pin down the precise positioning and hold the balance between “retro” and the contemporary — to talk about the past in today’s language without slipping into nostalgia.

What I did & the decisions I made
  • The project started with research that became its foundation: interviews with the museum team and analysis of reviews and museum archetypes. From this came the positioning “a monument to engineering ingenuity,” which became the anchor for every decision that followed.
  • The client was highly creative, with long feedback cycles. To keep that from turning into endless revisions, I kept all the work strictly within the agreed positioning and brief — as a result, edits were minimal and decisions were made at the first presentation.
  • Coordinated web production together with a second manager and made sure the final design matched the approved brief. When in-house resources fell short, I found and managed an external contractor — which is how we held the required quality.
  • The collaboration continued after delivery: the team attended the museum’s events and helped present the new identity.
Results
  • Strategy, identity, brand book, collateral, and website — a unified system for digital and offline.
  • The team ran joint events with the museum and launched a website with the new identity.

Case 04

Think Agency — repositioning and rebranding a digital agency

Role

Branding Producer

ONY · 2025–2026

Client

Think Agency (formerly Think Mobile) — a digital agency

Stakeholders

Founder (CEO), brand manager

Duration

5–6 months

Links

View on ONY · Sostav

Context & brief

By its fifth anniversary, Think Mobile had outgrown the mobile-marketing niche and expanded into adjacent areas — web performance, display and contextual advertising, and special projects. The old name and identity no longer reflected this scale. What was needed wasn’t a redesign but a rethink: new positioning, a new name, and a visual system from scratch — previously the agency had only a logo, a color, and a typeface. One particular nuance: the client was from the industry itself — the founder and brand manager understood the process well and assessed the work at a professional level, comparing it with world-class cases.

What I did & the decisions I made
  • We started with an immersion phase that resulted in a clear brief from the strategist. That brief became the single anchor for the whole team, setting the pace and quality of the work.
  • Headed off the risk of misalignment in a strong creative team in advance: I set up regular syncs with the creative director so the concept stayed unified across all stages. As a result, the team reached the client presentation with no internal discrepancies.
  • With a demanding, expert client I worked through process discipline rather than a mountain of iterations. The result — the project was approved almost at the first presentation, needing only minor color tweaks.
  • During the naming stage we explored options, but the client decided to keep the recognizable brand name.
Results
  • A full identity built on the metaphor of a fluid digital environment: a logo, a type pairing, a reimagined signature green, a system of soft fluid shapes, a photography style, and a brand book. We assembled flexible tools that scale easily to the agency’s new directions.
  • The rebrand strengthened the agency’s market position, made the value of its services easier to communicate, and opened the way into new directions.

Case 05

Brand-system refresh for a major bank (NDA)

Role

Branding Producer

ONY · 2025 — present

Client

A major federal-scale Russian bank (under NDA, pre-release)

Stakeholders

A corporate structure with multi-level approvals

Duration

1+ year, ongoing

Type

A comprehensive, multi-phase brand refresh

Context & brief

The bank came in with a task to update its brand system — not to build from scratch but to evolve the existing one: adjust the positioning and, on that basis, assemble a refreshed identity for several business directions at once. It is a large volume of work with a high quality bar, inside a corporation with multi-level approvals. The project’s defining feature is its length: it has been running for over a year and keeps growing with new tasks.

Project phases
  • Immersion & foundation: deep immersion in the project, a design audit, a brand-platform update, brand architecture, and tone of voice.
  • Identity: moodboard → typeface development → brand identifiers, then a 3D style, photography style, and 2D icons in parallel.
  • Guidelines: for icons, 3D, photography style, and broadcast, plus an AI guide — on generating 3D, photography style, and gradients with neural networks.
  • Direction identities: separate packs for different business directions — 168 3D objects, around 80 icons, and roughly 60 photos.
  • Brand book: assembling the final document.
What I did & the decisions I made
  • Ran a large multi-phase program: kept parallel streams in sync and made sure quality didn’t slip across large volumes.
  • Oversaw the development of the AI guide — steering the team and process so that the rules for generating 3D, photography style, and gradients were collected into a single working tool. This made the system scalable: assets can be reproduced without losing the style.
  • Grew the project through upsells within brand support: communication strategy, help with the internal release for the client’s team, merch, and other tasks.
  • For the client I was not just a contractor but the person who explains how the brand system works: I advised on the guidelines, showed how to apply them, and helped with both theory and practice.
  • When difficulties arose in adjacent areas — for example, on the website, where the manager was struggling — I stepped into client meetings myself, addressed objections, and turned the situation around. In the end I became a full curator of that direction together with a new website manager.
Results
  • Over a year of continuous work with one major client, and the project is ongoing.
  • A comprehensive, refreshed brand system: a signature typeface, 3D and photography styles, iconography, broadcast, a set of guidelines, an AI guide, business-direction identities, and a brand book.
  • A large volume of assets with consistent quality. Add-ons are currently in progress: another 121 3D objects for various directions and a pack of 291 icons.
  • After the main release we helped roll the brand out inside the company: updating internal tools (merch, presentations) and preparing the brand launch for employees — the bank is large, and it was important to introduce the new brand internally first.
  • The relationship grew into a long-term one: the client consistently returns with new tasks, and the collaboration keeps expanding.
